📚 How It Works

CO₂ Table

Rest time decreases each round while hold time stays constant. Trains your body to handle rising CO₂ — the main trigger of the urge to breathe. 8 rounds over ~20 min.

O₂ Table

Hold time increases each round while rest stays constant. Trains your body to consume oxygen more efficiently. Best done after CO₂ tables. 8 rounds over ~25 min.

REST Phase

Breathe slowly and deeply through your nose. Relax every muscle. Don't hyperventilate — just breathe naturally to recover between holds.

HOLD Phase

Take one final deep breath, then hold. Stay calm, keep still. Focus on relaxation, not the discomfort. The urge to breathe is just CO₂ — you have more oxygen than you think.

⚠ Safety Warning: Never practice breath-holds alone or in water. Always train on dry land, seated. Never train in a pool or ocean without a certified buddy watching you. Shallow water blackout can occur without warning.
